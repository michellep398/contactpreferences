Integrated Data Flow Process

1
Initial Setup: Promptly reads existing Practice Management data for baseline preferences. Patient records sync with Lead ID generation for CRM integration.
2
Master Control Established: Promptly becomes the authoritative source for all marketing consent preferences, overriding any conflicting data from other systems.
3
Consent Capture: Any patient touchpoint (website, portal, kiosk, staff override, or paperwork) captures consent changes and updates Promptly as the central hub.
4
Document Sync: Physical consent paperwork is digitally synced to patient record in Promptly for audit compliance and reference documentation.
5
Subject-Based Preference Distribution: Promptly pushes preference changes with specific subject selections (LASIK, Cosmetic Procedures, All) to corresponding CRM groups for targeted marketing campaigns.
6
Marketing Control: CRM receives preference updates from Promptly and immediately adjusts automated marketing campaigns accordingly.
7
Patient Control: Patients access self-service portal through Promptly to manage marketing and reminder preferences, with all changes flowing from this central hub.

Technical Integration Requirements

Promptly Database Enhancement: Add dedicated "Lead_ID" field, "Consent_Document_URL" field, and "Subject_Groups" array (LASIK, Cosmetic, All) to patient data tables for CRM group mapping
MDP/CRM Database Enhancement: Implement "Promptly_ID" field in patient records for integration while recognizing Promptly as master control for marketing consent
API Integration Layer: Develop uni-directional sync from Promptly to external systems ensuring Promptly maintains authoritative control over marketing preferences
Document Management System: Digital capture and storage of physical consent paperwork with direct linking to patient records for audit compliance and reference
Comprehensive Audit System: Track all consent changes with detailed timestamps, source attribution, staff identification, and document references for complete compliance trail
Dual-Track Compliance: Maintain separate preference tracking for marketing versus reminder/recall communications with distinct contact methods and Promptly-controlled rules
